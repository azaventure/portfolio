Projects

The Door — October 2025, Games 4 Social Impact 2025

This game was built for Pitt's annual Games 4 Social Impact game jam, of which the theme was migration. I worked in a group with two other people, and we decided to make a game based around taking a citizenship test, with increasing difficult and strange questions.


The game was built in Unity, and I was the lead developer. I wrote every script for the main game logic and designed the interactive "paper" object that the test is taken on.


The test was for a fictional country of which we created and developed the details. We then took all of the information we created and compiled it into a handbook that the player can use during the game to find the information they need for the test. The overall goal was to mimic the feeling of both taking a citizenship test, as well as taking a test when you're unprepared and the stress that accompanies that.


This game won the Judge's Choice award, chosen by the judges as the strongest overall project, and one that exemplifies the theme.


PRISLA Hypertext — April 2024, Narrative and Technology

This an interactive web experience and game I built that takes place on a wiki. The contents are based around a fictional organization that contains cryptids and anomalies, inspired by the SCP Foundation. The antagonist is an invisible force eating away at the organization's data. I used the tool Twine to create this and organize all of the articles. I wrote all of the articles myself and developed the UI myself, so this project blended my creative writing skills and programming skills.


At Pitt's 2025 Digital Media & Design Showcase, I won the Crowd Favorite award for this game, and was a finalist in contention for multiple other awards.


InSync — March 2025, Pitt Design Jam

I worked in a group for Pitt's inaugural Design Jam. We were given a week to develop a product that would accomdate for a variety of user profiles, with the theme of "Smart School". My group developed an app prototype named InSync that lets professors connect easier to their students and lets students connect easier with each other, both for studying and for connecting on a personal level. It featured easy creation of events for classes and an open forum where classmates can communicate in general or about specific assignments.


Tea Dragon Society: Amnesiac — November 2024, Digital Narrative and Interactive Design

I worked with a partner to make this game, a side-scroller based on the webcomic Tea Dragon Society. We were tasked with making a game based around a children's story, and chose this one due to its queer themes. The game features characters based on the story and gameplay elements that weave together plot points from the story. One of our top priorities was making a movement system that felt easy and fun to use. We used Unity, Git, and Aseprite to develop the game.

Accessibility

This portfolio website was built to be Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) compliant and employ universal design practices. The font is sans-serif and the colors are black on white to have easy visibility. The entire site is developed without the use of JavaScript, and is navigable even if the CSS is disabled, to enable the easy use of screen readers.
